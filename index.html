Four Diagnostic Categories

The model classifies each fundus photograph into one of four clinically distinct categories, with specific attention to high-cost missed diagnoses.

Normal

No clinically significant retinal pathology. Clear optic disc, uniform vasculature, intact macula.

Grad-CAM focus: macula & disc margins

Diabetic Retinopathy

Microaneurysms, dot/blot haemorrhages, hard exudates, cotton-wool spots, neovascularisation.

⚠ High-cost miss if classified as Normal

Cataract

Lens opacity causing diffuse haziness, reduced contrast, and blurred fundus detail in the photograph.

Grad-CAM focus: overall image haze pattern

Glaucoma

Enlarged cup-to-disc ratio, optic nerve head cupping, peripapillary atrophy, nerve fibre layer defects.

⚠ High-cost miss if classified as Normal

Workflow

How It Works

Step 1

Upload Image

Drag and drop or browse a colour retinal fundus photograph (JPEG, PNG, BMP, TIFF — up to 20 MB).

Step 2

AI Analysis

CLAHE preprocessing enhances vessel contrast. EfficientNetV2-S classifies across four categories with softmax confidence scores.

Step 3

Review Results

Examine the predicted class, per-class confidence breakdown, and the Grad-CAM heatmap showing which retinal regions drove the decision.

Architecture

Model & Pipeline

Network Architecture

1 EfficientNetV2-S ImageNet-21k pretrained
2 GlobalAveragePool 1280-d feature vector
3 BatchNorm + Dropout(0.4) regularisation
4 Linear(1280 → 512) + SiLU hidden layer
5 BatchNorm + Dropout(0.3) regularisation
6 Linear(512 → 4) + Softmax output

Training Strategy

Split 70% train / 15% val / 15% test (stratified)
Optimiser AdamW + CosineAnnealingLR
Loss Cross-entropy (label smoothing 0.1)
Unfreezing Progressive — epoch 10 & 20
Input 224 × 224 px, bicubic resize

Preprocessing Pipeline

CLAHE (green channel) Bicubic resize 224×224 ImageNet normalisation RandomFlip + Rotation ±15° ColorJitter Grad-CAM (final conv)

Explainable AI with Grad-CAM

Every prediction is accompanied by a Gradient-weighted Class Activation Map (Grad-CAM) heatmap, generated from the final convolutional layer. For glaucoma, the model attends to the optic disc region. For diabetic retinopathy, attention falls on haemorrhage and exudate areas. These visualisations allow ophthalmologists to validate whether model attention is clinically plausible.

Important Clinical Notice

This tool is a clinical decision support aid only. It must not be used as a substitute for examination and diagnosis by a qualified ophthalmologist or other licensed medical professional.

Model performance was evaluated on a balanced research dataset of ~4,000 fundus images. Real-world performance may differ due to imaging equipment, patient demographics, and disease prevalence.

Misclassification risk is highest for Diabetic Retinopathy → Normal and Glaucoma → Normal — both cases are flagged in the application output. Always obtain specialist review before any clinical action.
